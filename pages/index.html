What are they?

Japanese Quail are a species of game bird in the pheasant family. Frequently kept for meat and eggs, these diminutive birds are incredibly versatile (and CUTE!). They have been kept since at least the 12th century as domesticated animals according to Wikipedia Quail are very easy to care for, as you will see. They will live happily in a rabbit hutch or aviary. Some people even keep them in cages, however I personally feel more space is better for birds, even tiny ones like these!




How do I care for my birds?


As with any pet, birds have needs, and quail are no different! They need somewhere clean to live, I highly recommend a good hutch setup. They need food in the form of game bird feed, ideally with a higher protein level than for eg. chickens. They should ideally also be given leafy greens regularly, alongside some kind of grit that is rich in calcium.

If you are intending to breed your birds then it is usually a good idea to keep 1 male or "roo", to every 3 to 5 female "hens". You should usually be able to collect 1 egg per day per hen, which can then be put in an incubator for hatching or in the fridge for eating. Some people dont like the idea of eating fertilised eggs, but from my experience there isn't much difference.
